It is hard for us moderns to accept‚Äîconditioned, as we are, by Smokey Bear‚Äîbut fire is every bit as natural and inevitable in the American West as flooding in the Mississippi River Basin and hurricanes in Florida. Indigenous people lit wildfires all over the American West for millennia to manage land for this outcome‚Äîwith such success that, in the late 19th century, Anglo-American ranchers and even lumbermen adopted the practice.

The core mathematics of this science date to the early 1970s, when a Forest Service researcher named Richard Rothermel used small laboratory fires to produce equations expressing the relationship between wind speed, ground slope, and how fast a fire spreads.
